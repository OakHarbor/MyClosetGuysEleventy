Our Kitchen Cabinets are Durable and Functional

If you want your cabinet system to last for decades, avoid the cheap particleboard and one-size-fits-all approach that big box stores take. Saving a little bit of money now (which is not even guaranteed!) is not worth it in the long run.

Cheap cabinets will crack, warp, and sag, and in particularly unfortunate cases, they may fall out of the wall entirely.

Firstly, we measure your kitchen, garage, mudroom, or other space in great detail. We want to create a system that will be aligned with studs in the wall and your appliances, and we always create a perfect fit in our shop. Then we use high quality medium density fiberboard (MDF) -- this material is safe, workable to create a perfect fit, warp-free, and will last for decades. We also use professional hardware to hold it all together. We are committed to creating cabinet systems that will function well for many years to come.

Choose Your Kitchen Cabinet Style

Classic Shaker Cabinets

Everyone loves Shaker cabinets, and they always look great in historic areas like Alexandria. Shaker cabinets feature a recessed panel door, and you can add these cabinets in both traditional and modern kitchens. Many homeowners prefer a clean white look with their Shaker cabinets, although eggshell white and soft gray work as well. You can even go with navy blue, dark greens, or espresso paint - that's up to you.

Modern Slab Cabinets

Slab cabinets have flat, smooth doors without frames or panels, and they look great with high-gloss lacquer, matte neutrals, or natural wood grains. Slab cabinets look particularly good in contemporary kitchens -- stainless steel appliances, granite tiles, and other modern-looking features will pair well with slab cabinets. We are more than happy to send pictures.

Frequently Asked Questions

What Is the Process for Designing Custom Cabinets With Your Team?

We begin with a no-cost in-home consultation where we take measurements, talk through your needs, and learn how you want to use the space. What Types of Materials Do You Use for Custom Cabinets?

We typically work with high-quality commercial-grade medium density fiberboard (MDF). It's stronger, smoother, and more stable than lower-end plywood or particleboard, and it resists warping, cracking, and moisture issues over time. MDF also allows for a cleaner paint finish and tighter joinery. If you prefer solid wood, we can accommodate that request as well. Oak, walnut, maple, and other hardwoods are available depending on your project.

How Do Your Cabinets Compare to Big Box Store Options?

Big box store cabinets are mass-produced and come in set sizes, which can result in awkward gaps, filler strips, and wasted space. Many are made with thin particleboard and low-quality hardware. Ours are tailored to your space with thick, stable materials, better finishes, and built-in functionality. The difference is noticeable from the moment they're installed.
