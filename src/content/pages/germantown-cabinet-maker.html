Custom Kitchen Cabinets in Germantown Homes

We Think About Layouts, Function and Design

You need the right balance between drawer depth, vertical space, and accessibility. We'll discuss layout adjustments, including how to group drawers and doors based on your routines. A prep zone may need pull-out trash and compost bins below the counter. A baking area might benefit from tray dividers, soft-close rollout shelves, or vertical pull-outs for cooling racks.

Corner spaces can be made usable with kidney-shaped shelves or swiveling hardware that rotates fully out. We also offer upgrades like appliance garages, concealed lighting under cabinets, hidden charging drawers, and toe-kick storage that hides in plain sight.

Cabinet Construction and Material Options

Our cabinets are often built to size using solid plywood boxes with edge banding, not particle board or thin composite stock. You'll be able to select from a range of finishes including painted hardwood fronts, thermally fused laminate (TFL), and veneer panels. Paint-grade maple is a popular choice for clean white finishes, while oak, walnut, and cherry offer natural texture. We can also discuss all natural maple, oak, or other types of wood.

Hardware options include European concealed hinges, under-mount soft-close drawer slides, and full-extension glides. We'll walk through all of it during your consultation, with samples on hand to compare colors, textures, and durability.

Durable Garage Cabinet Systems

Heavy-Duty Storage

Garage storage should hold more than just paint cans. Our garage cabinet systems can be anchored to walls or freestanding depending on your space. We use high-strength melamine, powder-coated metal, and sealed plywood that won't chip or absorb moisture. Shelving and cabinets are reinforced to hold heavier tools, automotive supplies, and oversized gear.

We also offer integrated workbenches, vertical lockers for yard equipment, and pegboard panels for tool storage. Everything is cut to size and installed to avoid wasted floor space.

Wall-Mounted, Floor-Based, or Hybrid Systems

Some garages work better with a floating setup that keeps the floor clear. Others need deep base cabinets that double as support for a work surface. We'll look at how your garage is used and help determine the best structure for your needs. All drawers and doors come with high-use hardware designed for frequent, sometimes rougher handling.
