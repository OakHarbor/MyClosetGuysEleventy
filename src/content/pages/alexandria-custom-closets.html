Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Cabinets

How Does The Design Process Begin?

We start by scheduling a no-obligation consultation at your home. During that visit, we take accurate measurements, look at the layout of the space, and ask detailed questions about how you plan to use the cabinets. We also bring samples so you can see materials and finishes in person. Once we understand your goals, we create a full 3D rendering of the cabinet system. You'll be able to review and revise the design until it feels like a perfect fit for your space and your routine.

What Types Of Materials Can I Choose From?

We typically build our cabinets using high-quality commercial-grade MDF, short for medium-density fiberboard. It's denser and smoother than standard plywood or particle board, and it won't warp or split over time. This material gives us cleaner cuts, crisper edges, and a flawless surface for paint or laminate finishes. Lower-end materials often contain soft pockets or uneven layers that weaken over time---our MDF avoids those problems entirely. If you'd prefer natural wood, we also work with maple, oak, walnut, and other hardwoods upon request. We'll explain the differences during your consultation so you can make the right call for your project.

Can You Match Existing Trim, Finishes, Or Cabinet Styles?

Yes. If your home already has custom millwork, crown molding, or cabinet details you'd like to keep consistent, we can replicate or complement them in the new design. We've matched Shaker-style doors, inset panels, and custom paint colors in homes throughout Northern Virginia. Whether you're tying into a larger kitchen, an original built-in, or a specific trim profile, we can carry that look into the new system.

How Long Does The Full Process Usually Take?

Design typically takes one to two weeks, depending on how many revisions are needed. Once you approve the final layout and materials, we build everything in our shop and schedule the installation. Most projects are completed within three to four weeks from start to finish. We'll give you a clear timeline early on and keep you updated throughout the process so there are no surprises.

What Happens During The Installation?

We arrive with your full cabinet system pre-assembled and ready to install. Our team will carefully secure the cabinets to the walls, check every drawer and hinge, and adjust doors for proper alignment. We protect the surrounding area during the install and leave the space clean when we're done. If you have any questions or concerns during the walkthrough, we'll address them on the spot.

Hardware plays a major role in how the cabinets feel day to day, so we'll walk you through options that work for your layout and design preferences.

How Are Custom Cabinets Different From Store-Bought Options?

Most store-bought cabinets come in a few fixed sizes and rely on filler strips to bridge gaps. That often leads to awkward corners, uneven spacing, and materials that don't last. We build your cabinet system based on the actual measurements of your space. That gives you more usable storage, cleaner lines, and better durability. Our cabinets feel solid, align perfectly with the room, and age better than anything made with particle board or lightweight prefab parts.
