Forget Big Box Store Cabinet Systems

You can do better than a prefab cabinet system. The first issue with prefab systems is durability. Most are made with extremely cheap materials like particleboard or plywood, and these can warp, crack, and sag after just a few years. The hardware holding the system together matters as well -- cheap screws and hinges lead to the untimely demise of many cabinets. You should also take the installation into account, and not just because of looks! It can be dangerous to have a cheap cabinet system on your wall. Installing cabinets is an art form unto itself, and it needs to be executed perfectly.

Flat Panel or Slab Cabinets

Flat or slab cabinet doors look nice in traditional homes or contemporary spaces. They are often finished with a glossy look, but you can get as creative as you'd like with the color. If you have stainless steel features in your kitchen, these will look great. We can even make a slab cabinet system out of material that shows the wood grain -- this would look particularly good in a midcentury modern influenced house.

Choose Your Door Style

Full overlay doors completely cover the cabinet face, and they are the easiest to install. Partial overlay will allow about half of the cabinet face to show, and an inset structure means that the doors are fully flush with the inside of the cabinet face. We can work with any of these, and we'll chat with you about your preferences.

Beyond that, let us know if you want glass fronted doors to showcase china, or two-toned cabinets -- that's the benefit of a custom cabinet system.

Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Cabinets in Annapolis

What Is The First Step In Getting Custom Cabinets?

We start with a free in-home consultation. During that visit, we'll take detailed measurements of your kitchen or storage space, listen to your needs, and walk you through design options. You'll also receive a 3D rendering before anything is built so you can approve the layout, finish, and cabinet style with full confidence.

What Makes Your Cabinets Better Than Store-Bought Options?

Big box cabinets are made to fit anyone's home. Ours are made to fit yours. We don't rely on fillers or cut-to-fit adjustments on-site. Every part of your cabinet system is designed, measured, cut, and assembled for your exact space. This gives you a tighter fit, stronger construction, and a cleaner finish that holds up for years.

What Materials Do You Use In Your Cabinet Systems?

We typically use high-quality, commercial-grade MDF (medium density fiberboard) for its smooth surface, structural strength, and long-term durability. Unlike cheap plywood or particleboard, MDF won't splinter, warp, or buckle with age. If you prefer hardwood cabinetry, such as maple or oak, we're happy to accommodate that as well and will review all options during your consultation.

Can I Choose The Cabinet Style And Finish?

Yes. You can choose everything from door style (Shaker, slab, beadboard, etc.) to overlay type (full, partial, or inset), finish color, and hardware. We'll show you samples and help you select combinations that suit your space. Whether you want glass-front doors, two-tone cabinets, or a classic white finish, we'll make it happen.

Do You Only Build Kitchen Cabinets?

Not at all. We build custom cabinets for garages, home offices, libraries, pantries, bathrooms, and more. Many clients in Annapolis ask us to create full storage systems for multiple rooms in the home. We can design floor-to-ceiling storage, floating units, and wall-mounted systems for both utility and visual appeal.

Will I See The Design Before You Start Building?

Yes. Every client receives a 3D rendering of the cabinet layout before we start fabrication. This gives you the chance to visualize the result and request any changes before materials are cut. We want you to feel good about the design before we build a single piece.

We handle everything in-house. Our experienced installers will mount your cabinets securely, level each section, check the swing and alignment of every door, and clean up the workspace before they leave. Safety and precision are a big part of our reputation.
