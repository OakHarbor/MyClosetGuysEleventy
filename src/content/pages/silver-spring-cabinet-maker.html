We use durable materials that look good and last. Options include hardwoods like maple, oak, and birch, as well as painted MDF and high-grade laminates. How Our Cabinet Systems Compare to Store-Bought Options

Big box cabinets are made in standard sizes and shipped flat to fit any house. That approach might be cheaper upfront, but it often creates awkward gaps and shortens the life of the system. You'll also find yourself limited to a small group of finish and layout options.

We measure and build for your exact space. There's no filler material and no need to compromise on design. The cabinets are assembled in our shop and delivered ready for installation. That means tighter seams, straighter lines, and a layout that feels intentional.

Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Cabinets

How much input will I have on the final design?

Every project includes a full 3D rendering you can review and adjust before anything is built. You'll have input on layout, materials, finish, and hardware.

Do you offer recommendations if I'm not sure what will work?

Yes. Most clients come to us with a space that's not working, even if they don't know exactly what they want. We'll ask questions about how you use the room and help shape the design around those needs. You'll have access to a professional designer, not a showroom salesperson.

What are the advantages of using solid wood versus laminate or MDF?

Solid wood tends to have a longer lifespan and can be refinished later, but it's also more expensive. Painted MDF provides a smooth, modern look without wood grain, and laminate is durable in high-use areas like laundry rooms or garages. We'll help you choose based on your goals and budget.

Will the cabinets be built on-site or off-site?

We build everything in our shop and bring it to your home ready to install. That allows for cleaner assembly, tighter joints, and shorter install times.

Can you match existing trim or cabinetry?

Yes. We've matched existing cabinet styles, crown molding, and hardware in homes throughout Silver Spring. Just let us know what you're trying to coordinate with and we'll bring options.

How long does the full process take?

The design phase usually takes one to two weeks, depending on complexity. Once the design is approved, fabrication and installation typically take another two to three weeks. We'll give you a clear timeline during the consultation.
