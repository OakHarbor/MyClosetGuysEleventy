Custom Kitchen Cabinets With More Options and Better Materials

Practical Layouts

Kitchen storage needs to be efficient---not just attractive. We plan cabinet groupings around how you move through the room and what you store most. For some clients in Wheaton, that means switching from double-door lowers to deep drawers that hold cookware and small appliances. Others prefer pantry-style cabinets with rollout trays, vertical dividers for baking sheets, or tray pull-outs for oils and spices.

We also design around problem areas like corners, using options such as swiveling inserts, swing-out trays, or blind-corner drawers that give you access without wasted space.

Finishing and Construction Options

Cabinet boxes are usually built from furniture-grade plywood with thick edge banding for durability. We can review other material options like thermally fused laminate (TFL), natural wood veneer, or high-gloss acrylic, depending on your style goals and budget.

For fronts and drawers, we offer paint-grade maple, stained oak or cherry, slab doors, or Shaker profiles. You'll be able to compare styles and finishes during the design meeting, using real samples, not printed catalogs. Our hardware selections include soft-close hinges, undermount slides, and full-extension drawer glides that perform well for years without adjustment.

Cabinets That Transform the Garage

Built for Heavy Loads and Rougher Use

Garage storage often has to handle weight, moisture, and daily wear. We use thicker materials and sealed surfaces to hold tools, yard equipment, and bins without sagging or warping. Our cabinets can be mounted to the wall or installed with toe-kick bases to rest on the floor.

We can include wide drawers for tools, lockers for tall items, and open shelving for gear that doesn't belong behind closed doors. If you need a work surface, we'll build that into the plan with enough clearance for storage underneath and space above for lighting or pegboard.

Wall-Mounted, Freestanding, or Mixed Systems

Some garages in Wheaton work best with a floating cabinet system that keeps the floor open. Others benefit from deep base cabinets that act as both storage and a built-in workbench. We help decide what fits the layout best by walking through how you use the space now and what's missing. Cabinet sizes, shelving heights, and door swing directions are all customized to fit your preferences.

Custom Offices and Media Wall Cabinetry

In home offices or dens, we install low cabinets with file drawers, upper units with closed storage, and shelving for books and displays.
